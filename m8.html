⚛️ Konsep Dasar Radikal Bebas⚛️ Basic Concept of Free Radicals

Radikal bebas (free radical) adalah spesies kimia yang memiliki satu atau lebih elektron tidak berpasangan (unpaired electron). Ciri-ciri utama:

  • ⚡ Sangat reaktif — elektron tunggal cenderung mencari pasangan.
  • ⚡ Bersifat netral secara muatan (bukan ion).
  • ⚡ Memiliki 3 elektron valensi ikatan + 1 elektron bebas pada atom karbon.
  • ⚡ Dilambangkan dengan titik (•) setelah simbol atom: CH₃•, C₂H₅•
Pembentukan Radikal: Terjadi melalui homolisis — pembelahan ikatan kovalen di mana masing-masing atom mendapat 1 elektron.
A–B → A• + B•

A free radical is a chemical species with one or more unpaired electrons. Key characteristics:

  • ⚡ Highly reactive — the single electron seeks to pair up.
  • ⚡ Electrically neutral (not an ion).
  • ⚡ Has 3 bonding valence electrons + 1 lone electron at the carbon atom.
  • ⚡ Symbolized by a dot (•) after the atom: CH₃•, C₂H₅•
Radical Formation: Occurs via homolysis — covalent bond cleavage where each atom receives 1 electron.
A–B → A• + B•

🏆 Stabilitas Radikal Karbon🏆 Carbon Radical Stability

Stabilitas radikal karbon ditentukan oleh jumlah gugus alkil yang terikat pada atom karbon berradikal. Semakin banyak gugus alkil → semakin stabil, karena:

  • 🔹 Hiperkonjugasi: Orbital σ C–H pada gugus alkil yang berdekatan dapat berinteraksi (overlap) dengan orbital yang mengandung elektron tidak berpasangan, mendistribusikan densitas elektron dan menurunkan energi sistem.
  • 🔹 Efek Induksi: Gugus alkil bersifat sedikit elektron-donor, memberikan kerapatan elektron tambahan pada atom karbon radikal.

Carbon radical stability is determined by the number of alkyl groups attached to the radical carbon. More alkyl groups → more stable, because:

  • 🔹 Hyperconjugation: The σ C–H orbitals of adjacent alkyl groups can overlap with the orbital containing the unpaired electron, distributing electron density and lowering system energy.
  • 🔹 Inductive Effect: Alkyl groups are slightly electron-donating, providing extra electron density to the radical carbon atom.

🔁 Reaksi Substitusi Radikal🔁 Radical Substitution Reactions

Substitusi radikal adalah reaksi di mana atom atau gugus pada molekul digantikan oleh radikal. Contoh paling umum adalah halogenasi alkana (klorinasi / brominasi) menggunakan cahaya UV atau panas.

Reaksi umum: CH₄ + Cl₂ →hν CH₃Cl + HCl

Radical substitution is a reaction where an atom or group in a molecule is replaced by a radical. The most common example is alkane halogenation (chlorination / bromination) using UV light or heat.

General reaction: CH₄ + Cl₂ →hν CH₃Cl + HCl

➕ Reaksi Adisi Radikal➕ Radical Addition Reactions

Adisi radikal terjadi pada senyawa tak jenuh (alkena/alkuna). Contoh paling penting: adisi HBr pada alkena dengan adanya peroksida (inisiator radikal) yang mengikuti aturan anti-Markovnikov.

Tanpa peroksida (ionik/Markovnikov): CH₃CH=CH₂ + HBr → CH₃CHBrCH₃ (2-bromopropana)
Dengan peroksida (radikal/anti-Markovnikov): CH₃CH=CH₂ + HBr →ROOR CH₃CH₂CH₂Br (1-bromopropana)

Radical addition occurs on unsaturated compounds (alkenes/alkynes). The most important example: HBr addition to alkenes in the presence of peroxide (radical initiator) following the anti-Markovnikov rule.

Without peroxide (ionic/Markovnikov): CH₃CH=CH₂ + HBr → CH₃CHBrCH₃ (2-bromopropane)
With peroxide (radical/anti-Markovnikov): CH₃CH=CH₂ + HBr →ROOR CH₃CH₂CH₂Br (1-bromopropane)

This is the controlling factor of regioselectivity — not charge as in ionic mechanisms. HCl and HI generally do not follow this pattern due to different energetics.

🔗 Reaksi Eliminasi Radikal🔗 Radical Elimination Reactions

Eliminasi radikal melibatkan pemecahan ikatan β (β-scission) — fragmen radikal pecah pada posisi β terhadap elektron tidak berpasangan menghasilkan alkena dan radikal baru. Ini adalah proses kebalikan dari adisi radikal.

Radical elimination involves β-scission — a radical fragment breaks at the β position relative to the unpaired electron, generating an alkene and a new radical. This is the reverse of radical addition.

⚙️ β-Scission (Fragmentasi β)

R–CH₂–CH•–R' → R–CH₂• + CH₂=R'
(radikal β memecah menghasilkan radikal primer + alkena)

Contoh penting dalam industri: cracking minyak bumi — rantai panjang alkil radikal mengalami β-scission berulang menghasilkan alkena pendek dan bahan bakar.

Contoh: •CH₂CH₂CH₂CH₃ → CH₂=CH₂ + •CH₂CH₃

β-Scission juga penting dalam:

  • 🔹 Degradasi polimer (rantai polimer putus menjadi monomer)
  • 🔹 Pirolisis biomassa
  • 🔹 Oksidasi lipid (ketengikan lemak)

⚙️ β-Scission (Beta Fragmentation)

R–CH₂–CH•–R' → R–CH₂• + CH₂=R'
(β radical cleaves to give primary radical + alkene)

Important industrial example: petroleum cracking — long alkyl radical chains undergo repeated β-scission to yield short alkenes and fuels.

Example: •CH₂CH₂CH₂CH₃ → CH₂=CH₂ + •CH₂CH₃

β-Scission is also key in:

  • 🔹 Polymer degradation (chain breaks back to monomers)
  • 🔹 Biomass pyrolysis
  • 🔹 Lipid oxidation (fat rancidity)

🔑 Kaitan Stabilitas & Reaktivitas:🔑 Stability–Reactivity Link: Tiga tipe reaksi radikal semuanya dikendalikan oleh prinsip yang sama: intermediet radikal yang terbentuk haruslah yang paling stabil. All three radical reaction types are governed by the same principle: the radical intermediate formed must be the most stable. This governs substitution regioselectivity (Br), addition regiochemistry (anti-Markovnikov), and the direction of β-scission.

• Tertiary (3°): 3 alkyl groups → most stable
• Secondary (2°): 2 alkyl groups → stable
• Primary (1°): 1 alkyl group → less stable
• Methyl (0°): no alkyl groups → least stable
This order parallels carbocation stability, opposite to carbanion stability.

The reaction of propene (CH₃CH=CH₂) with HBr in the presence of peroxide (ROOR) gives the main product...
📚 Pembahasan:📚 Explanation: Adisi HBr dengan peroksida berjalan melalui mekanisme radikal (anti-Markovnikov):
• Peroksida menghasilkan Br• (bukan H⁺).
• Br• beradisi ke C terminal (C1) propena, menghasilkan radikal sekunder di C2 (lebih stabil daripada radikal primer di C1).

• Peroxide generates Br• (not H⁺).
• Br• adds to the terminal C (C1) of propene, giving a secondary radical at C2 (more stable than a primary radical at C1).
• That secondary radical then abstracts H from HBr → product is 1-bromopropane.
This is the opposite of ionic addition (no peroxide) which gives 2-bromopropane (Markovnikov) via a secondary carbocation.

Seorang mahasiswa menyatakan: "Adisi HBr pada alkena selalu mengikuti aturan Markovnikov, tidak peduli kondisi reaksinya." Apakah pernyataan ini benar?
A student states: "HBr addition to alkenes always follows Markovnikov's rule, regardless of reaction conditions." Is this statement correct?
📚 Pembahasan:📚 Explanation: Pernyataan mahasiswa tersebut TIDAK BENAR. Adisi HBr pada alkena dapat berlangsung melalui dua mekanisme berbeda yang menghasilkan produk berbeda:
1. Ionik (Markovnikov): Tanpa peroksida, HBr terdisosiasi menjadi H⁺ dan Br⁻. H⁺ beradisi ke C yang lebih sedikit H (menghasilkan karbokation lebih stabil) → Br⁻ masuk ke C karbokation → produk Markovnikov.
2. Radikal (Anti-Markovnikov): Dengan adanya peroksida (ROOR), Br• terbentuk. Br• beradisi ke C terminal (menghasilkan radikal sekunder lebih stabil) → produk anti-Markovnikov.
Hanya HBr yang menunjukkan efek peroksida ini — bukan HCl atau HI karena perbedaan energetika ikatan. The student's statement is NOT TRUE. HBr addition to alkenes can proceed through two different mechanisms giving different products:
1. Ionic (Markovnikov): Without peroxide, HBr dissociates into H⁺ and Br⁻. H⁺ adds to the C with fewer H atoms (generating a more stable carbocation) → Br⁻ attacks the carbocation C → Markovnikov product.
2. Radical (Anti-Markovnikov): With peroxide (ROOR), Br• forms. Br• adds to the terminal C (giving the more stable secondary radical) → anti-Markovnikov product.
Only HBr shows this peroxide effect — not HCl or HI due to bond energetics differences.

• The bond at the β position (two atoms from the radical center) breaks homolytically.
• It produces: an alkene (from the fragment losing the electron) + a new, smaller radical.
• Example: •CH₂CH₂CH₃ → CH₂=CH₂ + •CH₃
This is the reverse of radical addition and is important in thermal petroleum cracking. Option B is termination, C is H abstraction (substitution propagation), D is Cl₂ addition (not β-scission).
